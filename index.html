About

I am a final-year MA Economics student at the Delhi School of Economics — one of India's most rigorous programmes in economics. My training has given me deep foundations in econometrics, causal inference, and quantitative analysis, which I apply to questions at the intersection of finance, policy, and human behaviour.

At the Reserve Bank of India, I analysed credit growth and portfolio stress dynamics across 70 NBFCs — applying regression modelling and correlation analysis to understand what drives non-performing assets. The work gave me hands-on exposure to financial sector data at scale.

Beyond numbers, I am drawn to the story data tells. Whether it is the hidden cost of a family health shock on a child's education, how restaurant ratings are shaped by non-linear interactions, or what post-COVID volatility means for hedge fund strategy — I look for research questions grounded in reality. I bring the same rigour to everything: code, writing, or a national case competition.

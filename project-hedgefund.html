Revitalising Hedge Funds in a Post-COVID, High Volatility World

Financial StrategyPortfolio ManagementQuant MethodsML Regime SwitchingESGFinalist — 400+ Teams

Presented by Team Wednesday at KaryaNeeti, NSUT's national case competition. We proposed a specialised niche hedge fund strategy for India's emerging market, combining quant equity long-short, global macro, volatility strategies, and alternative data signals — with ML-based regime switching and a reformed fee structure. The fund targets 15% annualised returns with a Sharpe Ratio of 1.35, outperforming both the Nifty 50 and Global ETF benchmarks.

Competition Context

KaryaNeeti is a national-level case competition on business and financial strategy, organised by the Entrepreneurship Cell at NSUT, Delhi. Our team — Team Wednesday — reached the finals out of 400+ competing teams, presenting to a panel of judges on hedge fund strategy, portfolio construction, and risk management.

The problem statement: traditional hedge funds are underperforming passive benchmarks post-COVID, fee structures face pressure from passive ETFs, and India's hedge fund industry remains underdeveloped despite being a $5.7 trillion global market with rising capital inflows. How should a new fund be structured to revitalise the model?

Why Specialised Niche Over Multi-Strategy?

We argued that for an emerging market fund targeting India's high-growth sectors, a specialised niche strategy offers deeper expertise, competitive moats from domain knowledge, and early-stage access to transformative sectors — while a multi-strategy approach would require operational complexity and capital scale that a new fund cannot efficiently deploy.

Core Portfolio & ML-Based Regime Switching

The core portfolio allocates across four strategy types: Quant Equity Long-Short (40%), Global Macro in FX/Rates/Commodities (25%), Volatility Strategies via Options/VIX (20%), and India-specific Alternative Data Bets (15%). Crucially, these allocations shift dynamically based on an ML-detected market regime.

Reformed Fee Structure

We proposed moving beyond the traditional "2 and 20" model, which faces mounting pressure from passive ETF growth and investor demands for transparency.

Lower management fee (1.75%): Signals cost discipline while covering core operational expenses.

Performance hurdle: Performance fee charged only on returns above NIFTY50 benchmark — investors pay for alpha, not beta.

High-water mark: Fee applies only to profits exceeding previous peaks, preventing double-charging in volatile markets.

Long-term incentives: Fee reductions for commitments exceeding 3 years, aligning fund growth with investor success.

Technology & Regulatory Framework

On the technology side, we proposed reinforcement learning for trade execution (minimising slippage, as used by Citadel and Two Sigma globally), leverage of NSE co-location infrastructure for HFT, and a blockchain-based investor dashboard showing live NAV, risk exposure, and ESG compliance scores — inspired by Bridgewater's radical transparency ethos.

For regulation, the fund would operate as a SEBI Category III AIF, with minimum investment of ₹1 crore per investor targeting sophisticated and institutional capital. Ethical guardrails include zero tolerance for insider trading, use of only legal and anonymised alternative data sources, and real-time compliance dashboards with tech-enabled breach alerts.
